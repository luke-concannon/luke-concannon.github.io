The Learning Plan of an Aspirational Junior Dev!

The (DRAFT) Vision


Firstly, I have an immediate need to upskill to drive the development of the tech product we are offering as part of the business I am involved in. Having come from a paper based system almost 10 years ago, it has been digitised and is now evolving into and online tool. Heaps of potential and a customer base to drive its development, I just need the skills to take it to othe next level / build a team that can help me take it there!


Secondly, I want to develop a set of transferable skills that will allow me to explore other directions / projects in the future. My current situation was/is a pretty niche opportunity in that I have grown into a family business over the years after tarting up a few spreadsheets for the business. I love the job as it affords me lots of flexibility and freedom of choosing my own direction.


Across both of these goals, I wish to develop a clearer understanding of my own value system. What exactly is it I’m trying to achieve with the things I am building? How do I want my work to contribute to something bigger than just myself and my family?


Some (non-technical!) Things I'd like to Develop:

  • My ability to collaborate in real-time with a group of people
  • My ability to communicate technical concepts and direction in simple and compelling terms
  • My ability to identify and communicate the key things any given stakeholder needs to hear i.e. not providing needless tech context for stakeholders that don’t need it!
  • My ability to delegate and collaborate to achieve a common goal. I work in a silo and I would like to build a team over the coming years

Me, Myself, and I


A little about me as a learner, through mine very own eyeballs!


Things I'm Proud of:

  • I consider myself a jack of all trades, able to pivot and learn what needs to be learnt to journey towards the greater goal
  • I can deep dive on topics I'm drawn towards and can become almost obsessive about new hobbies/industries
  • Asking questions is a place I feel comfortable, which is incredibly useful in a learning context
  • Beginners mindset is also a comfortable place for me to exist. I'm well practiced in starting on new pathways and find it natural to play padawan to someone else’s Jedi
  • Feedback and constructive criticism are not things I find difficult to take on board.

Things I'm working on:

  • Being a jack of all trades, I'm also a master of none!
  • Direction - I fall back on the idea that your career / life ebbs and flows in mysterious ways and you should trust in this organic approach, but sometimes I also worry that I'm not purposeful enough or lack direction or conviction
  • I lack confidence working in groups. Public speaking I find particularly uncomfortable.
  • By nature I need time to be creative / to solidify learning. I struggle in real-time group contexts e.g. group brain-storming sessions or collaborative workshops. I find I need to go away on my own, process what is required, then bring my findings back to the group.

My Bootcamp Manifesto


- ROUTINE

I benefit hugely from routines and plan to establish a daily routine whilst living in Wellington for the 9 weeks


- GOAL SETTING

I will be clear with myself around what exactly I am aiming to achieve in any given working block. I have a tendency to go deep down rabbit holes and work in a very linear fashion.


- BALANCE

Man it is so easy to work for 12 hours straight on a screen when you are doing something you love and can immediately see the results! I will commit to breaking up my efforts, using pomodoro timer and establishing a routine that involves commitments to analog activities outside of the binary existence! Sports, friends, walks, maybe a few friday night beersies with Piwakawaka crew!


- RELATIONSHIPS

I am looking forward to getting to know my EDA cohort and believe that in any given situation, the relationships you build are the most important factor in working productively and safely with other learners. Understanding other people, or at least empathising with the fact we all come from different backgrounds and have different goals/needs, provides a good foundation to working with others effectively


- COMMUNICATION

We are privileged to live in an era where talking about how you feel is not only acceptable but a priority. I hope to communicate honestly and openly with others on the course.


- SUSTENANCE

I will maintain caffeination and a full belly at all times. No one wants to see a hangry Luke.


- TAKING BREAKS

The joys of stopping, doing a few star jumps / cracking a bad joke with a friend / taking a quick walk and then revisiting whatever is getting to you is a bloody marvelous technique!


- SHARING PROBLEMS

‘A problem shared is a problem halved!’ I’m loving the open source feel to the EDA course so far and can see the value in coming back to the idea of open source in more contexts than just code!


- ASKING FOR HELP

This is definitely something that is good to practice. Again it fits the open source approach to things. Help from a real person is untouchable in its value!
