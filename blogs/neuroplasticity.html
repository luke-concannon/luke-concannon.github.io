Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

"Neurons that Fire Together, Wire Together"


Your brain is more than just a 3 pound ball of jelly that I imagine tastes delicious (based on what the brains of other beasts taste like... apologies to all the vegans out there). It is a complex map of neural pathways with firing synapses left, right and centre. It's a veritable disco of electrical activity responding to the things you ask it to do each and every moment of your life. Choose to kick a football and the unique set of neural pathways required to make that happen will light up like a christmas tree. Do it again tomorrow and it will light up again. Do it again the next day and that specific combination of neural pathways is starting to get pretty well versed in firing - so much so that the neurons begin to wire together, making the pathway more efficient. A path well trodden if you will


Does that mean we can become more cleverer?


The concept of Intelligence is fraught with contradiction and conflicting ideaologies. EQ... IQ... OMG...WTF. We can however agree that there are diffferent types of intelligence, and what is becoming clearer through research is that many of these intelligences are flexible i.e. NOT FIXED. This follows on logically from the concept of neuroplascticity and being able to literally shape our brains by doing and thinking.


The Hallowed Era of the Growth Mindset


So we have this proven concept of our brains as a malleable chunk of clay that we can shape as we so desire. But we also have a conflicting idea that we are born with a set of fixed cognitive attributes, our intelligence, that sets our capacity to learn and achieve throughout our lives. What do we do when we have 2 conflicting ideas? FIGHT, FIGHT FIGHT! We put them in a ring and see who wins (or gather reems of peer reviewed research and try to scientifically prove one over the other). In the corner of flexible intelligence was Carol Dweck, an American psychologist who proposed that the idea of fixed mindset was absurd when in actuality we have the opportunity to enhance our learning. Her idea of Growth Mindset cast egg on the faces of all those that perceived their intelligence as set from birth. It turns out all they did was limit their own capacity to learn and become more capable, intelligent human beings! YES, your own belief in Growth vs Fixed Mindset can influence your ability to harness neuroplascticity in your favour! Kind of like if you convince yourself you may see a unicorn in People's Coffee you are more likely to actually see that unicorn. Become the Potter of your own Intelligence


How do we take advantage of our new found cognitive plasticity? Here are some totally achievable tips to becoming the potter of your own intelligence! (your brain is the clay - for those that thought the analogy too abstract):


  • REPETITION! REPETITION! REPETITION! - Practicing things is hugely effective in building those neural pathways. But we mainly already knew that.
  • CHALLENGE and NOVELTY - Doing new and challenging things helps create novel connections in your brain, developing a more diverse map of neural pathways.
  • AWARENESS - Just actively considering the fact that you can shape your own learning / intelligence / capability gives you a more potent ability to take advantage of neuroplascticity. Go on, think it now.
  • BALANCE - Hard work is a given. But how good are we at resting, relaxing, enjoying the birds and the bees (literally and euphemistically)? Also, sleeping, meditating and conscious attention. All great and useful tools in our quest to learn the things we previosly considered out of reach!
