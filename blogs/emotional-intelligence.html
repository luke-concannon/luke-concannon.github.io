Emotional Intelligence: The Not-so-Secret Weapon of the Person Seeking Growth


What is Emotional Intelligence?


According to the binary know-it-all (wikipedia):


“Emotional intelligence (EI or EQ) is most often defined as the ability to perceive, use, understand, manage, and handle emotions.”


It’s a simple but profound recognition of the essential role emotions play in the narrative of all that we experience.


Collaborating with a colleague on a project? - both of you will have an underlying emotional journey you are traveling whilst completing this project; working alone to debug an issue with your app? - you will have an underlying emotional journey you are traveling whilst completing this debugging. Working in a large group to introduce and get to know each other? Every single person has an underlying emotional journey they are traveling whilst experiencing this moment.


Emotional Intelligence refers to a person’s awareness of this emotional journey in themselves and in others. EQ also refers to the ability to shape your behaviour appropriately based on your awareness of the emotional content of your own and others people behaviour.


An example. A pair of front-end devs walk into a bar (best start to a not-joke ever)... They’re both working on independent projects and are sharing their projects with one another as colleagues and buddies. Dev A knows that Dev B recently got the bash from a client around a website they’d designed. Instead of Dev A launching into showing off their project to Dev B, they purposefully commit time to being a positive audience for Dev B, providing confidence boosting compliments and making positive statements. Dev A is showing their emotional intelligence here. Some may say that this is just being a good friend, and it is!


Emotional intelligence is the practice of bring some of those qualities of awareness we have when we are operating as peak-friend into the rest of our lives.


How is it Different to IQ (Intelligence Quotient)?


Intelligence quotient or IQ is a score received from standardized assessments designed to test intelligence. IQ relates directly to intellectual pursuits such as the ability to learn as well as understand and apply information to skill sets. IQ covers logical reasoning, word comprehension and math skills. People with higher IQ can think in abstracts and make connections by making generalizations easier.


Why is Emotional Intelligence Relevant to Me?


It is important because it is threaded between every moment we ever experience, on our own and with others. We are emotional beings. It is part of what makes us human and to not practice the skills involved with EQ is to avoid the joy of experiencing a part of who we are.


Also, if you ever want to have anything to do with other human beings, you better get practicing those EQ skills, or hope the people on the receiving end of your lack of emotional awareness is an absolute jedi in EQ, acceptance, non-judgment and compassion!
